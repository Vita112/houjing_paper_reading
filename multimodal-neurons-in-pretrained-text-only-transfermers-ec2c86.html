Multimodal Neurons in Pretrained Text-only Transfermers

Probing for Multimodal

Takeaway

  • Image prompts(output of linear projection layer) do not encode interpretable semantics, which is to some extend consistent with LiMBer paper;
    • proved by exp. 3.1
    • no significant difference between random embeddings and image prompts in terms of CLIPScore distribution
    • suggesting that Translation between modalities occurs inside transformer;
  • multimodal neurons are reliable detectors of concepts, since its preferential activation on particular categories of images;
    • proved by exp. 3.2
    • 对于每一种特定的image category，被激活的neurons是不一样的。
  • multimodal neurons causally affect caption output;
    • proved by exp3.3

3. Experiments

Experiment 1: linear projection layer has no competence of transferability between modalities

KS test on comparing CLIPScore distribution of image prompts with that of random embeddings ⇒ no significant difference

but CLIPScore distribution of image prompts with that of five COCO nouns ⇒ significant difference

image prompts are mapped onto 5 nearest tokens, which are called decoded image prompts.

左边的实验结果表明：这些image soft prompts does not encode semantics that can be interpreted by LLMs.

也就是说这个linear projection layer has no competence of transferability between modalities.

Experiment 2: neurons are selectively active for images containing particular concepts

do not extract a set of general visually attentive units, but rather units that are specifically tied to image semantics
